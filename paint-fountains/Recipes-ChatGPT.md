**Approach 2 — “Showy & Accessible”**
-------------------------------------

**Silicone Oil vs Mineral Oil**

**Goal:** Two glossy, oil-based liquids that layer cleanly and flow like thick paint.  
**Visual Effect:** Vivid, slow-moving blobs or ribbons of color that never truly mix.

### **Ingredients**

*   150 mL **Silicone Oil (500 – 1000 cSt)**
*   150 mL **Mineral Oil (Paraffin Oil)**
*   Oil-based or silicone-safe colorants (mica or grease-soluble dyes)
*   _Optional:_ 0.5 – 1 g **fumed silica** to thicken the thinner oil

### **Procedure**

1.  **Color each phase:**
    *   For silicone oil, stir in mica powder (≈ 0.5 % by weight) or a few drops of silicone pigment.
    *   For mineral oil, use 2 – 4 drops of grease-soluble food color or oil paint.
2.  **Adjust viscosity:**
    *   If mineral oil feels runny, disperse ≈ 1 % fumed silica and stir until slightly creamy.
3.  **Combine:** Pour both into a clear vessel and tilt to observe swirling separation.

**Tuning range:** Viscosity ratio of silicone : mineral ≈ 1 : 1 to 1.5 : 1 for balanced flow.

**Approach 4 — “Kitchen Chemistry”**
------------------------------------

**Water + Glycerin Mixture vs Mineral Oil**

**Goal:** Safe, opaque “paint-like” fluids using food-grade ingredients.  
**Visual Effect:** Milky, pastel lower layer (water-glycerin) beneath a translucent oil top.

### **Water Phase**

*   100 mL **Distilled Water**
*   100 mL **Vegetable Glycerin (≈ 50 : 50)** → range 40 : 60 to 70 : 30 for viscosity tuning
*   2 – 3 g **Titanium Dioxide (TiO₂)**
*   Water-soluble food color (few drops until desired hue)
*   _Optional:_ 0.2 – 0.5 g **Xanthan Gum** for extra body

### **Oil Phase**

*   200 mL **Mineral Oil**
*   1 – 3 drops **Oil-based food dye** or a pinch of mica powder

### **Procedure**

1.  Dissolve TiO₂ in a bit of warm water, then add glycerin and color. Whisk smooth.
2.  If needed, sprinkle xanthan and blend until it forms a syrupy paint.
3.  Tint the mineral oil separately.
4.  Pour one phase over the other slowly → clear two-layer system (heavy white base below).

**Tuning range:** Glycerin content ↑ → thicker and slower; TiO₂ 1 – 3 % controls opacity.

**Approach 5 — “Artist’s Recipe”**
----------------------------------

**Acrylic Medium vs Silicone Oil + Fumed Silica**

**Goal:** Two viscous, paint-textured fluids that behave like real acrylic colors but never mix.  
**Visual Effect:** Creamy paints that form veins and marbling patterns, then separate cleanly.

### **Phase A – Water-Based**

*   100 mL **Acrylic Gloss (or Pouring) Medium**
*   0.5 – 1 g **Xanthan Gum** (≈ 0.5 – 1 %)
*   Pigment or acrylic paint for color (1 – 5 %)
*   Distilled water if needed to loosen texture

### **Phase B – Oil-Based**

*   100 mL **Silicone Oil (500 – 1000 cSt)**
*   2 – 4 g **Fumed Silica (≈ 2 – 4 %)** → gel consistency
*   Mica powder or silicone-compatible dye for color

### **Procedure**

1.  **Thicken acrylic phase:** Add xanthan slowly while stirring; adjust with water for desired flow.
2.  **Prepare silicone gel:** Sprinkle fumed silica into oil while mixing until evenly dispersed (avoid dust).
3.  Color each phase.
4.  Layer or stir together gently to observe viscous immiscible interaction.

**Tuning range:**

*   Fumed silica 2 % = runny cream; 5 % = stiff gel.
*   Xanthan 0.3 – 1 % fine-tunes acrylic body.

* * *

### **Reuse and Storage**

Let mixtures stand until layers separate, then decant each into airtight jars.  
Avoid cross-contamination by using dedicated utensils for water-based and oil-based phases.
