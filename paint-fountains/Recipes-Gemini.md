Recipe 1: Silicone vs. Hydrocarbon (Approach 2)
-----------------------------------------------

This recipe is an "all-oil" system, which makes it very stable (no evaporation or mold) and easy to source. The main challenge is matching the viscosities, as mineral oil is naturally thinner than the high-viscosity silicone.

### Phase A: Silicone Phase

*   **Base Fluid:** Silicone Oil (PDMS)
*   **Viscosity:** Start with a **500 cSt to 1000 cSt** grade. The 1000 cSt will give a more "syrupy" flow.
*   **Colorant:** Oil-soluble pigments, artist's oil paint, or oil-soluble candy colors.

### Phase B: Hydrocarbon Phase

*   **Base Fluid:** Mineral Oil (also called Paraffin Oil or White Oil). Look for a "heavy" or "medicinal grade" if possible, as it will be slightly more viscous.
*   **Colorant:** Oil-based food coloring (specifically those made for chocolate/candy).

### 🔑 Key Areas for Experimentation

1.  **Viscosity Matching:** The silicone oil (Phase A) will likely be much thicker than the mineral oil (Phase B). You have two options to create a good visual match:
    *   **Option 1 (Easiest):** Use a lower-viscosity silicone oil (e.g., 350-500 cSt) to more closely match the mineral oil.
    *   **Option 2 (Thickening):** If you want both phases to be thick, you must thicken the mineral oil. As your text suggests, test dissolving **petroleum jelly** into the mineral oil. Start with a range of **2% to 10% by weight**, warming the oil gently to dissolve it.
2.  **Liquid Ratios:** Start with a **1:1 volume ratio** of Phase A to Phase B to see how they interact, then adjust the volumes for your fountain's design.

Recipe 2: Water/Glycerin vs. Mineral Oil (Approach 4)
-----------------------------------------------------

This is the "kitchen chemistry" approach. It's inexpensive and uses easy-to-find ingredients. The key is balancing the water/glycerin phase to get the perfect "paint-like" opacity and thickness.

### Phase A: Water "Paint" Phase

*   **Base Fluids:** Distilled Water + Glycerin (Glycerol).
*   **Opacifier:** Titanium Dioxide (TiO2) powder (food or cosmetic grade).
*   **Colorant:** Standard water-soluble liquid or gel food coloring.

### Phase B: Oil Phase

*   **Base Fluid:** Mineral Oil (or Baby Oil).
*   **Colorant:** Oil-based food coloring (for chocolate/candy).

### 🔑 Key Areas for Experimentation

1.  **Water Phase Viscosity:** This is your main control. Experiment with the **Glycerin to Water ratio**.
    *   Start at **50:50** for a thick liquid.
    *   Increase up to **70:30 (Glycerin:Water)** for a very thick, syrupy consistency.
2.  **Water Phase Opacity:** Adjust the **Titanium Dioxide** powder.
    *   Start with **1% by volume** (e.g., ~1 ml of powder for 100 ml of liquid).
    *   Increase up to **3% by volume** for a very opaque, paint-like look.
3.  **Oil Phase Viscosity (Optional):** This phase will be much thinner. If the visual difference is too jarring, you can thicken the oil by dissolving **1% to 5% by weight of beeswax or paraffin wax**. This requires gently heating the oil until the wax melts, then letting it cool.

Recipe 3: Acrylic Medium vs. Thickened Silicone (Approach 5)
------------------------------------------------------------

This is the "artist's recipe" and will give the most "paint-like" texture, as both phases are designed to be thick and opaque. It requires more precise mixing.

### Phase A: Water "Paint" Phase

*   **Base Fluid:** Acrylic Gloss Medium.
*   **Thickener:** Xanthan Gum powder.
*   **Colorant:** Acrylic ink, concentrated liquid pigment, or good-quality acrylic paint.

### Phase B: Silicone "Paint" Phase

*   **Base Fluid:** Silicone Oil (a **500 cSt or 1000 cSt** grade).
*   **Thickener:** Fumed Silica (e.g., Aerosil 200).
*   **Colorant:** Artist's oil paint or dry pigment powders (e.g., iron oxides).

### 🔑 Key Areas for Experimentation

1.  **Water Phase Viscosity:** Add **Xanthan Gum** to the Acrylic Medium.
    *   Start at **0.5% by weight** (e.g., 0.5g powder per 100g of medium).
    *   Increase up to **1.5% by weight** for a very thick gel.
    *   **CRITICAL:** Add the xanthan gum _very slowly_ while stirring constantly (ideally with a hand mixer) to prevent clumps.
2.  **Silicone Phase Viscosity:** Add **Fumed Silica** to the Silicone Oil.
    *   **Wear a dust mask.** Fumed silica is extremely light and fluffy and should not be inhaled.
    *   Start by testing **2% by weight** (e.g., 2g powder per 100g of oil).
    *   Increase up to **6% by weight** to create a thick paste. This will require significant stirring to fully disperse. Using a 500 cSt silicone oil will be easier to mix than a 1000 cSt oil.
3.  **Ratio:** A **1:1 volume ratio** is the best starting point.

I would recommend starting your tests with **Recipe 2 (Water/Glycerin)** as it's the cheapest and easiest to adjust, which will help you understand the flow dynamics before moving to the more complex recipes.
